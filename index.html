About me

I’m a PhD student in computer science at the University of Michigan–Flint, where I explore the crossroads of software engineering and artificial intelligence. My research looks at how we can make machine learning systems more reliable, maintainable, and easier to evolve over time. I’m especially interested in SE4AI, AI4SE, and software evolution, and how rigorous methods from software engineering can be applied to solve practical problems in AI systems.

Alongside my research, I worked as a teaching assistant, where I enjoy helping students master programming and systems concepts by breaking them down into clear, approachable lessons. As a research assistant, I dive deeper into projects that investigate the quality, robustness, and long-term impact of software used in AI.

What excites me most is the mix of research and problem solving. Whether I’m testing new ideas, running experiments, or working on ways to make AI systems more dependable, I’m motivated by the chance to push technology forward and make a lasting impact.
